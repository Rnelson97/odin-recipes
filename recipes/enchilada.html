Enchilada Recipe

Description

Beef enchiladas are a savory and satisfying Mexican dish that combines tender, seasoned beef with a flavorful tomato-based sauce, all wrapped up in soft corn tortillas. To prepare this delicious recipe, start by browning ground beef with onions and garlic, then add a blend of spices like cumin, chili powder, and paprika to infuse it with rich, smoky flavors. After simmering the beef in a tomato sauce until it's tender and well-cooked, you'll fill each tortilla with a generous portion of the beef mixture, roll it up, and place it seam-side down in a baking dish. Top the enchiladas with more sauce and a generous amount of shredded cheese, then bake until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown. Serve these beef enchiladas with a dollop of sour cream, a sprinkle of fresh cilantro, and perhaps some sliced jalapeños for an irresistible meal that's sure to please your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 10-12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (typically a blend of cheddar and Monterey Jack)
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, chopped fresh cilantro, sliced jalapeños

Steps

  1. Brown 1 pound of ground beef in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and minced garlic to the beef, and cook until they become translucent.
  3. Stir in 1 tablespoon of chili powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin, 1/2 teaspoon of paprika, salt, and pepper to taste. Cook for another minute to let the spices bloom.
  4. For the sauce, combine 1 can (15 ounces) of tomato sauce, 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes, 1 teaspoon of chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon of cumin, 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a separate bowl.
  5. Simmer the beef mixture in the tomato sauce until the beef is tender and the flavors meld together.
  6. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  7. Take 10-12 corn tortillas and warm them briefly in the microwave or on a griddle to make them pliable.
  8. Place a portion of the beef mixture in each tortilla, roll them up, and arrange them seam-side down in a baking dish.
  9. Pour the remaining tomato sauce over the enchiladas and sprinkle 2 cups of shredded cheese on top.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
